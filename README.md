# prapancame 🌀

*The proliferating thicket of concepts that obscures direct-knowing — Nāgārjuna's prapañca received as a cosmic decree: the mind's runaway multiplication that hides suchness.*

prapancame is two true roots welded into one word: the Sanskrit **prapañca** (प्रपञ्च, "expansion, manifoldness, elaboration"), which Madhyamaka Buddhism names as the problematic conceptual-multiplication that distances a knower from suchness, and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So prapancame is prapañca held as an ordinance — the thicket received as a conditioned feature of mind, not chosen. It names the *runaway-multiplication* specifically: where concepts breed concepts, the overlay thickens, and direct-grasping becomes invisible. It is not thinking and not careful-discrimination; those keep their proper-place. It is the over-elaboration that outruns the work — the doom-scroll, the anxious spiral, the analysis that paralyzes.

prapancame names a **condition** of mind, not a particular thought. Modern tongues only blur at it — "overthinking," "rumination," "analysis paralysis" all reach for it and fall short — while Madhyamaka articulates the structural-feature with operational precision. Its light is that naming it begins to loosen it, and that it points always past itself toward the quieting (prapañca-upaśama) that lets suchness manifest. Its shadow is anti-intellectualism — the lazy move that calls all hard thinking "just prapañcame" and crowns laziness as peace, betraying its own root, which insists the quieting is not anti-intellectual. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.10/10**.
